Youth Boxing in State College Legacy Boxing & Combat Sports Academy

Help your child build confidence, discipline, and fitness through structured youth boxing and combat sports classes. Our program is safe, supportive, and led by experienced coaches who focus on helping every kid grow with confidence.

Trusted, Structured Boxing for Kids Ages 8–16

Our Youth Program is about more than just boxing – it's about giving young athletes the tools to grow stronger both inside and out. Classes are led by USA Boxing certified coaches and supported by accomplished student-athletes from the nationally ranked Penn State Boxing Team. These mentors help guide drills and serve as role models your child can look up to.

Kids will learn proper boxing fundamentals, footwork, coordination, and self-control in an environment that's welcoming and age-appropriate. Every session is designed to build character, focus, and resilience – all while having fun and staying active.

There's no sparring or contact required. We use skill-building games and controlled partner drills like Shoulder Tag to develop awareness, movement, and timing in a fun and safe way.

Our team works closely with each child, offering patient instruction and positive reinforcement. Whether your child is shy, energetic, athletic, or totally new to sports, they'll find encouragement and belonging here.

Our partnership with the Penn State Boxing Team brings accomplished college boxers with a high-level of experience and a genuine passion for mentoring. They lead drills, offer encouragement, and help every student feel confident – whether it's their first day or they're returning for another season.

Benefits for Your Child

  • Develop confidence, coordination, and focus
  • Learn self-discipline and how to handle pressure
  • Stay active in a positive, structured environment
  • Guidance from experienced, caring coaches
  • Safe, contact-free training with coach-approved progression

Youth Combat Sports Summer Camp (Ages 7–16)

Give your child a summer full of learning and movement. Our Youth Summer Camp runs June 24 through July 17, every Tuesday, Wednesday, and Thursday from 12:00 PM to 4:00 PM. Campers will stay active while learning boxing, kickboxing, fitness, and nutrition – plus film study that helps kids understand technique and strategy in a way that's fun and easy to follow.

Every day includes structured training, games, positive coaching, and snacks. It's an engaging and supportive way to build confidence, learn new skills, and stay moving over the summer break.
