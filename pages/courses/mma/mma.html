Frequently Asked Questions about MMA Classes

Do I need any experience or to be in shape before joining?

Not at all. Our MMA program is designed for complete beginners as well as experienced martial artists. You'll start with the basics and build up your skills and fitness over time. Many students join with no prior experience or fitness background – some are Penn State students who have never tried martial arts before – and they quickly improve with our beginner-friendly approach. If you're already in shape or have trained elsewhere, that's great, but it's not a requirement. Come as you are, and we will help you progress at a pace that's right for you.

What should I wear or bring to class? Do I need my own gear?

For your first few classes, just wear comfortable athletic clothing (t-shirt, shorts or leggings) and bring a water bottle. We train on padded mats, so wrestling shoes are recommended. We have loaner gloves and protective gear (like focus mitts, Thai pads, etc.) available for beginners. As you continue training, we recommend investing in your own boxing/MMA gloves, 180-inch wrist wraps, a mouthguard, and shin guards for striking – but there's no need to have these on day one. Our staff can advise you on what gear to get when you're ready.

Is MMA training safe for beginners? Will I have to spar or get hit?

Safety is our top priority. Beginners will not be thrown straight into hard sparring. Our classes incorporate drilling and light contact exercises to build your skills and confidence. Any sparring or live rolling we do is closely supervised by the coach and kept at a level appropriate to your experience. You'll wear protective gear (gloves, mouthguard, etc.), and more experienced partners will work with you safely. Injuries are rare and we take care to ensure everyone trains smart. As you gain experience, you can choose to participate in more intense sparring rounds, but it's always optional and introduced gradually. In short, MMA training at Legacy is as safe as any other sport – you'll be challenged, but we keep it controlled and beginner-friendly.

Can Penn State students join your MMA classes?

Absolutely! We welcome Penn State students – in fact, a large part of our membership is made up of PSU students and staff. Our gym is only a few minutes from campus, and easily accessible by car or CATA bus. (There's plenty of free parking at our facility if you drive.) Class times in the evenings and on Saturdays are scheduled to fit a student's busy class timetable, so you can train after your academic day is done. Coach Derek's role as the PSU Boxing Club head coach also means you'll be training under someone very familiar with the Penn State community. Whether you're an undergrad or grad student, you'll find our schedule and location very convenient. Training MMA is a great outlet from studies – and you might even meet fellow Penn Staters in class!

Do you offer MMA classes for teens or kids?

Yes, our MMA program is open to teens (typically ages 16 and up) as well as adults. Teenagers often train alongside adults in our regular classes, with coaching adjusted as needed to their age and skill level. It's a fantastic way for teens to build confidence, get in shape, and learn self-defense in a controlled setting. For younger children below the early teen years, we offer separate youth martial arts programs (through our academy's youth classes) that focus on foundational skills in a fun, safe environment. If you're unsure which program is best for your child, just reach out and we'll help you find the perfect fit.

Will I get to compete or fight if I train here?

That's completely up to you. Many of our members train for personal development, fitness, and fun without ever competing, and that's 100% okay. However, if you do have goals to compete in MMA, boxing, Muay Thai, or BJJ, we can certainly help you on that path. Coach Derek has experience preparing fighters for amateur competition and can guide dedicated students through fight camps and advanced training when the time comes. We've had students participate in local boxing matches and grappling tournaments. When you're ready and have built a solid foundation, we'll support you in testing your skills in the ring or cage. Competition is optional – you won't be pressured to fight – but if it's something you aspire to, Legacy Boxing & Combat Sports Academy is equipped to train you for it.

How do I get started and try a class?

Getting started is easy – your first class is free! Simply contact us to schedule your free introductory class, or drop in a few minutes early before any scheduled MMA session to sign up on the spot. Our staff will have you fill out a quick waiver, then we'll introduce you to the coach and get you oriented. You'll be able to jump into the class and train with the group on day one. We're confident that once you try a class, you'll love the experience. If you have any other questions or concerns before coming in, feel free to call or email us. We're here to help and look forward to meeting you!
