Muay Thai Striking

Known as the "art of eight limbs," Muay Thai trains punches, kicks, knees, elbows, and clinch control as one system — the most complete striking art for MMA. In our Muay Thai classes in State College you will build power, timing, and clinch control drilled with a real MMA context, not as an isolated sport. No prior experience needed.

Kosen Judo & Grappling

Kosen Judo is the branch of judo where a takedown flows directly into sustained ground fighting rather than resetting — training takedowns, pins, chokes, and joint locks as one sequence. It is the historical root of Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu and the most MMA-applicable grappling system available. Coach Roth holds a USA Judo brown belt and is a certified USA Judo instructor.

Frequently Asked Questions about MMA Classes

Do I need any experience or to be in shape before joining?

Not at all. Our MMA program welcomes complete beginners – many of our members are Penn State students who had never trained before joining. You'll start with fundamentals and build skills and conditioning over time. Prior fitness or experience is a bonus, not a requirement. Come as you are.

Is MMA training safe for beginners? Will I have to spar or get hit?

Safety comes first. Beginners focus on drilling and light-contact exercises – you won't be thrown into hard sparring. Any live work is supervised and matched to your experience level. Protective gear is always worn. More intense sparring is introduced gradually and only when you're ready. It's always optional.

Can Penn State students join your MMA classes?

Absolutely. A large part of our membership is Penn State students and staff. We're minutes from campus with free parking, and evening and Saturday class times fit a student schedule. Coach Derek also leads the PSU Boxing Club, so you're training under someone who knows the Penn State community.

Do you offer Muay Thai classes in State College?

Yes. Muay Thai striking is a core part of our MMA curriculum, not an add-on. Every MMA class includes Muay Thai technique — kicks, knees, elbows, and clinch work — taught with proper structure and MMA application. If you are specifically looking for Muay Thai classes near Penn State or in State College, our MMA membership gives you exactly that, combined with grappling and boxing in one complete program.

Is Kosen Judo similar to jiu-jitsu? What grappling do you teach?

Very similar in outcome, different in origin. Kosen Judo is the branch of judo where takedowns flow directly into sustained ground fighting — pins, chokes, and joint locks — without resetting. BJJ was derived from it directly. If you have been looking for grappling or jiu-jitsu classes in State College, our Kosen Judo curriculum covers that ground and integrates directly with the striking in our MMA program.

How do I get started and try a class?

Your first class is free. Contact us to schedule your intro class, or drop in a few minutes early before any MMA session. We'll get you oriented, introduce you to the coach, and have you training with the group on day one.
