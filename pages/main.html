The Legacy Experience

Ready to train like a real fighter? At Legacy Boxing and Combat Sports Academy you'll build authentic boxing and mixed martial arts skills, boost your fitness, and develop the discipline that drives lasting results inside and outside the gym. We offer structured boxing classes and authentic mixed martial arts training designed for beginners to advanced fighters alike.

Our Boxing Classes teach the same techniques used by Golden Gloves competitors, All-Americans, and national champions. Sparring is never required and only introduced when you're fully prepared, with safety and readiness as the top priority. You'll build real technique, conditioning, and fight IQ through focused drills like Shoulder Tag, a Cuban boxing drill that safely builds speed, coordination, and sharp defensive awareness in a fun, engaging way. Our program is ideal whether you're training for amateur boxing, preparing for your first fight, or simply looking for a real boxing gym instead of a cardio-focused class.

Our MMA Classes bring striking and grappling together into one complete system – this is real mixed martial arts, taught the right way. Each session combines techniques from boxing, kickboxing, Muay Thai, Judo, Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu, submission grappling, and catch wrestling. You'll work through striking-only and grappling-only drills, learn clinch work and takedowns, and practice defending and attacking under pressure. Every class builds timing, control, and true fight-ready skills, whether you're new to MMA or leveling up your game.

Our Youth Boxing Classes, open to ages 8 to 16, offer a safe and structured boxing class for kids where they build confidence, coordination, and lifelong skills. With monthly memberships and convenient drop-in options, families can stay consistent without needing to commit to seasonal camps. All instruction is led by USA Boxing certified coaches who prioritize safety, motivation, and age-appropriate training.

Memberships start at $75 per month for adult boxing or MMA, and $60 for children. Private sessions, facility rentals, and drop-in options are also available. Your first class is free, so you can experience what sets our athletes apart before committing. Whether you're looking to compete or just get in shape, our gym is where your boxing journey begins.

Meet Coach Roth

Coach Derek Roth is the Head Coach of the Penn State University Boxing Club Team since 2019, which is currently the top civilian boxing program in the United States. In 2023, he was named National Collegiate Boxing Association (NCBA) Coach of the Year. He brings this same level of commitment and expertise to Legacy Boxing and Combat Sports Academy.

His background includes:

  • 30 years of martial arts experience
  • 25 years of boxing experience
  • Training in Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu since 2009
  • Ongoing study of Kosen Judo
  • Competition experience in MMA, Kickboxing, and Muay Thai
  • Former Pennsylvania Golden Gloves boxer

Coach Roth also holds certifications in:

  • Submission Grappling Instruction
  • Kickboxing and Muay Thai Instruction
  • Personal Training
  • Nutritional Coaching

Coach Roth designs and leads all Academy programs based on years of coaching national champions. Through classes, private sessions, and continued mentorship, he helps students of all levels achieve their goals in fitness, competition, and personal development.

"The National Collegiate Boxing Association (NCBA) was formed in 1976 and is a group member of USA Boxing. The NCBA philosophy and rules provide a safe outlet for student boxers with little or no experience in the sport. The organization emphasizes the teaching of fundamental novice boxing skills while providing a safe, positive, and educational experience for student athletes. The NCBA encourages participation at the recreational, instructional, intramural, and competitive amateur boxing levels."

Coach Marc Hessel

Marc Hessel created and led the photography program in the Penn State University School of Visual Arts for 33 years, retiring as Professor Emeritus. He was awarded a National Endowment Fellowship in the Arts for his Puerto Rico portfolio, where he also developed a lifelong photography project. During that time, he had the privilege of photographing world champions Félix “Tito” Trinidad and Iván “Iron Boy” Calderón, as well as heavyweight contender Fres Oquendo, while they trained in Puerto Rico. This work sharpened his ability to observe detail – a skill that later carried over into coaching.

In 2005, when amateur boxer Héctor Rosario turned professional, Rosario invited Coach Marc to join Team Rosario as an assistant trainer. He went on to support the team not only in boxing preparation but also by supervising nutrition, drawing on his background in science as a pre-med undergraduate and graduate researcher in biology. From 2012 to 2017, he also assisted Pennsylvania pro Grayson Blake in his training.

In 2014, he was invited to help coach the Penn State Boxing Club's competition team, where he continues to serve as assistant coach.

Although he never boxed himself, Coach Marc has been an avid student of the sport since childhood. While his peers collected baseball cards growing up in New York, he collected boxing cards – a sign that boxing was always part of his destiny.
