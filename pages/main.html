Coach Marc Hessel

Marc Hessel created and led the photography program in the Penn State University School of Visual Arts for 33 years, retiring as Professor Emeritus. He was awarded a National Endowment Fellowship in the Arts for his Puerto Rico portfolio, where he also developed a lifelong photography project. During that time, he had the privilege of photographing world champions Félix “Tito” Trinidad and Iván “Iron Boy” Calderón, as well as heavyweight contender Fres Oquendo, while they trained in Puerto Rico. This work sharpened his ability to observe detail – a skill that later carried over into coaching.

In 2005, when amateur boxer Héctor Rosario turned professional, Rosario invited Coach Marc to join Team Rosario as an assistant trainer. He went on to support the team not only in boxing preparation but also by supervising nutrition, drawing on his background in science as a pre-med undergraduate and graduate researcher in biology. From 2012 to 2017, he also assisted Pennsylvania pro Grayson Blake in his training.

In 2014, he was invited to help coach the Penn State Boxing Club's competition team, where he continues to serve as assistant coach.

Although he never boxed himself, Coach Marc has been an avid student of the sport since childhood. While his peers collected baseball cards growing up in New York, he collected boxing cards – a sign that boxing was always part of his destiny.
